						2. Legal Conditions
				  2.1 Registration of vehicles, vehicle papers

1. (2P) As the vehicle owner, what change must you report tot he relevant licensing authority? A Change to
a)	my name
b)	my home address

2. (2P) You are the new owner of a licensed vehicle. What do you have to do? I have to
a)	present the licensing authority with proof oft he existence of liability insurance for the vehicle
b)	notify the licensing authority oft he change in ownership

3. (2P) You have sold your vehicle which is still registered. The buyer has paid. What must you do?
a)	Inform the registeration centre immediately of the buyer’s name and address
b)	Hand over the vehicle registeration certificate (Fahrzeugschein) and vehicle registeration book(Fahrzeugbrief) 
or permit for use (Betriebserlaubnis) to the buyer against reciept.

4. (2P) What is important with regard to the official registeration plates?
a)	They must be always be easily legible
b)	They must be securely attached at the appropriate places
c)	The rear registeration plates must be lit up in darkness

5. (2P) What are you allowed to change on the official registeration plate?
a)	Nothing

6. (2P) What documents do you have to carry with you, if you want to drive a compulsory registerable vehicle?
a)	My driving license
b)	The registration certificate Part 1 (Motor Vehicle Registeration)

							2.2 Vehicle Inspections
1. (2P) How can you tell when you need to bring your vehicle in for ist next vehicle road worthiness test?
a)	By looking at the inspection tag on the rear license plate
b)	By reading the entry made in the registration certificate Part 1

2. (3P) An inspection has been performed because of a technical modification made to your vehicle. What are you obliged to do? I have to
a)	carry the inspection report or confirmation in my vehicle, and have the registration in certificate Part 1 corrected if necessary

 3. (2P) You have bought a second hand car. The official inspection (Hauptuntersuchung) has just been carried out. When it is due for ist 
next official inspection?
a)	After two years

4. (2P) You have left your registered caravan trailer for six months during the winter on a campground. What applies as far as the 
official inspection (Hauptuntersuchung) is concerned?
a)	The date indicated on the test stamp must be observed

5. (3P) The suspension of your motor vehicle has been lowered and an expert opinion must be obtained. When is this required?
a)	Immediately after the alteration

6. (3P) A Technical modification to your vehicle means that the operating license is extinguished. What consequences should you 
anticipate if you nevertheless use the vehicle on public roads?
a)	A public authority fine
b)	The loss of my insurance cover
c)	An entry into the Centre Register of Road Traffic Offenders

						2.3 _ Insurances, loss of Insurance cover
1. (3P) You want to use a motor vehicle on public roads. What must you observe?
a)	The motor vehicle must be roadworthy
b)	The motor vehicle must be covered by liability insurance

2. (3P) What is required when your vehicle is no longer covered by motor liability insurance? The vehicle
a)	May no longer be used in road traffic
b)	Must be de-registered at the registration centre
